First, we're failing our children.

Today's high-pressure environment is harming our children's mental health in three key ways.

Too Much Pressure

Our school system puts intense academic pressure on kids from a very young age.

Digital & Social Pressure

Constant screen time and social media makes growing up even harder.

Not Enough Playtime

Children are losing the free, creative playtime that is essential for development.

Here is our 3-point plan to fix it.

Our 10-year plan focuses on three achievable changes that will make the biggest difference.

1

Smaller classes, more attention

Reduce primary school class sizes to 25 students or fewer. This allows teachers to give each child the individual attention they need, adapt to different learning styles, and build stronger relationships.

2

Make the PSLE optional

Replace the single high-stakes exam with a "through-train" system that lets children progress to secondary school without intense national pressure, reducing anxiety and focusing on continuous learning.

3

More support in every school

Ensure every school has a strong team of trained counselors, therapists, and learning support specialists so children can get immediate access to professional help.

Our 10-year plan starts today.

Our comprehensive report details the evidence and provides a practical roadmap for changing our primary schools for the better. This is a long-term strategy that requires immediate action.
